The Spectator

Their hands that move with foreign coordination;
they put My pen to paper.
blind, to their sole Spectator.

Their blood, the colour of a tragedy, latent;
they pull My sleeve, impatient.
time, a fast-flowing glacier.

Their touch, giving way to a phantom sensation;
they take in My forced breath.
lest it frost my glass prison...







and wake Me up.

'That is not dead which can eternal lie. And with strange aeons even death may die.' - H. P. Lovecraft
